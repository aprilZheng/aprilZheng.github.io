Graduate Studies

During my graduate studies, I explored the potential of Virtual Reality (VR) as a medium for simulation and creativity. The two projects below demonstrate my experience in designing immersive environments and conducting user-centric studies.

Driving Simulator

In the Driving Simulator project, I developed a Unity3D-based VR system to study AR-enhanced driving scenarios. The project introduced three driving modes—"see-through," dashboard, and traditional—and involved an experiment with 17 participants to evaluate perception, parking, and driving performance.

Graffiti Simulation in Virtual Reality

The Graffiti Simulation in Virtual Reality project focused on collaborative creativity using Meta Quest 2. This application allowed users to create graffiti in shared virtual spaces with features like virtual spray cans, real-time communication, and pigment mixing. The design emphasized accessibility and dynamic interactions, offering an engaging and intuitive experience.
