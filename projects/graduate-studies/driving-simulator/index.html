Augmented Reality Driving Simulator - Graduate Studies

This project explored the potential of Augmented Reality (AR) to address vehicular blind spots by simulating real-world driving scenarios using Virtual Reality (VR). Leveraging Unity3D, SteamVR, and a steering wheel, I developed a driving simulator with three enhanced visibility modes: Traditional Mode, AR Transparent Mode.

Participants engaged in perception, parking, and driving tasks, with the following findings:

  • Traditional Mode provided the most accurate perception results.
  • AR Transparent Mode showed promise with the shortest parking times.
  • Bird's Eye View Mode was the most user-friendly and preferred mode overall.

To simulate the AR "see-through" effect, I placed a transparent material plane in front of the VR camera, representing AR smart glasses.

While the results were promising, some participants experienced physical discomfort (e.g., dizziness and nausea) caused by prolonged use of the VR headset. This emphasizes the need for ergonomic refinement in VR equipment for future studies.
